Notes for you (not part of the prompt)
* The DPA is opinionated, not generic. I drafted it to reflect how Allotly actually works — that prompts and completions are not processed by Allotly, that AI providers are not Sub-processors of Allotly (the end-user picks them at runtime), and that customer-supplied API keys are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. These are protective positions for Allotly. A standard SaaS template would have lumped AI providers into the Sub-processor list, which would create obligations you don't actually have.
* Why a separate Sub-processors page. The DPA references allotly.ai/subprocessors rather than listing Sub-processors inline. This is the standard pattern (Stripe, Vercel, OpenAI, Anthropic all do it this way) and it lets you update the Sub-processor list without re-issuing the DPA. Make sure both pages ship together — a DPA pointing to a 404 looks worse than no DPA.
* The four infrastructure placeholders. Be honest about what you actually use. If your stack is Vercel + Neon + Stripe + Resend + Sentry (a common Next.js startup combo), say so. Reviewers and procurement teams cross-check this against your security posture; vague entries like "various cloud providers" raise more questions than specific ones.
* Governing law in §15. If you're UK-incorporated, "England and Wales" is the standard choice. Avoid choosing a jurisdiction Allotly has no real connection to — it's unenforceable and looks like template-filling.
* What this gets you with Anthropic. Once /dpa is live, paste the URL into the "Data Processing Agreement URL" field in the submission form. That single field flips your submission from "small startup, work in progress" to "B2B-ready, has done the homework" — meaningful signal at the review margin.
* What it does not get you. This is a v1 DPA — defensible for a small SaaS at your stage. It is not a substitute for legal review once you start signing six- or seven-figure Enterprise contracts. The first time a Fortune-500 procurement team red-lines it, get a lawyer to review their changes against this baseline.
